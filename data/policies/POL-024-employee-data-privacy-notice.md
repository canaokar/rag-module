# Employee Data Privacy Notice

## 1. Scope and Legal Basis

This privacy notice explains how the Bank collects, uses, and protects the
personal data of its employees, contractors, and temporary workers. It is
provided in accordance with the Bank's obligations under the UK GDPR and
the Data Protection Act 2018.

The Bank acts as the data controller for employee personal data. The Data
Protection Officer may be contacted at dpo@bank.example.com.

## 2. Categories of Personal Data

2.1 The Bank processes the following categories of employee personal data:

- Identity data: full name, date of birth, national insurance number,
  passport or identity document details
- Contact data: home address, telephone numbers, personal and work email
  addresses
- Employment data: job title, department, employment history, salary,
  benefits, performance reviews, disciplinary records
- Financial data: bank account details (for payroll), pension contributions,
  tax codes
- IT and security data: system access logs, email communications (business
  accounts), CCTV footage
- Special category data (where applicable): health and disability
  information, trade union membership

## 3. Purposes and Lawful Bases

3.1 The Bank processes employee data for the following purposes:

- Performance of the employment contract: payroll, benefits administration,
  work allocation, performance management
- Legal obligation: tax reporting (PAYE, NIC), health and safety compliance,
  pension auto-enrolment, right to work checks
- Legitimate interests: security monitoring, business planning, fraud
  prevention, internal communications
- Consent: optional health and wellbeing programmes, employee surveys,
  photographs for internal directories

## 4. Data Sharing

4.1 Employee data may be shared with the following categories of recipients:

- Payroll and benefits providers
- Pension scheme administrators
- HMRC and other government agencies (as required by law)
- Occupational health providers
- Group companies (for intra-group administration)
- Regulators (FCA, PRA) where required for fitness and propriety assessments

4.2 Data will only be shared with third parties where a lawful basis exists
and appropriate contractual safeguards are in place.

## 5. Retention

5.1 Employee personal data is retained for the duration of employment plus
6 years following termination, unless a longer retention period is required
by law.

5.2 Recruitment data for unsuccessful candidates is retained for 12 months
from the date of the decision.

## 6. Employee Rights

6.1 Employees have the following rights under the UK GDPR: right of access,
right to rectification, right to erasure (in limited circumstances), right
to restrict processing, right to data portability, and the right to object
to processing.

6.2 Requests may be submitted to the Data Protection Officer. The Bank will
respond within 25 business days.

## 7. Monitoring

7.1 The Bank monitors employee use of IT systems, including email, internet
access, and telephone usage, for security, regulatory compliance, and
conduct risk purposes.

7.2 CCTV monitoring is in operation at Bank premises for security purposes.
Footage is retained for a maximum of 90 days.

## 8. Governance and Oversight

The Data Protection Officer is responsible for ensuring compliance with this
notice. The Human Resources function is responsible for ensuring that
employee data processing activities align with the stated purposes.

## 9. Review and Amendment

This notice must be reviewed at least annually or following a material change
in employment data processing practices.
