# Current Account Terms and Conditions

## 1. General Terms

These terms and conditions govern the provision of current account products
and services by the Bank to eligible customers. By opening an account, the
customer agrees to be bound by these terms.

## 2. Account Opening

2.1 Accounts may be opened by individuals aged 18 or over who are resident
in the United Kingdom and who satisfy the Bank's identification requirements.

2.2 A minimum initial deposit of GBP 1 is required for standard current
accounts. Premium current accounts require a minimum monthly funding of
GBP 1,500.

2.3 The Bank reserves the right to decline an account application without
providing reasons, in accordance with applicable law.

## 3. Interest and Charges

3.1 Credit interest on current account balances is paid monthly at a variable
rate, currently set at 1.5% AER. The Bank will provide at least 14 business
days notice of any rate change.

3.2 Overdraft interest is charged at 19.9% EAR (variable). An arranged
overdraft limit of up to GBP 5,000 is available subject to credit assessment.

3.3 Returned item fees of GBP 15 will be charged for each payment rejected
due to insufficient funds, up to a maximum of GBP 60 per month.

3.4 There is no monthly account fee for the standard current account. The
premium current account has a monthly fee of GBP 15, which includes travel
insurance, mobile phone insurance, and preferential overdraft rates.

## 4. Payments and Transfers

4.1 The Bank supports the following payment methods: Faster Payments (up to
GBP 250,000 per transaction), CHAPS (no maximum), Direct Debits, standing
orders, and international transfers via SWIFT.

4.2 Payment cut-off times: Faster Payments are processed within 2 hours on
business days. CHAPS payments must be submitted by 15:30 for same-day
processing.

4.3 The customer is responsible for ensuring sufficient funds are available
to cover scheduled payments. The Bank may decline payments where insufficient
funds are available.

## 5. Closing an Account

5.1 Either party may close the account by providing at least 20 business days
written notice. The Bank reserves the right to close an account immediately
where fraud or financial crime is suspected.

5.2 Upon closure, any outstanding balance will be transferred to a nominated
account or issued as a banker's draft.

## 6. Complaints

6.1 Complaints may be submitted via telephone, in branch, by post, or through
the Bank's website.

6.2 The Bank will acknowledge complaints within 5 business days and aim to
resolve them within 8 weeks. If the customer is not satisfied, they may refer
the complaint to the Financial Ombudsman Service.

## 7. Governing Law

These terms are governed by the laws of England and Wales. Disputes will be
subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the English courts.

## 8. Definitions

"AER" means Annual Equivalent Rate and illustrates what the interest rate
would be if interest was paid and compounded once each year.

"EAR" means Effective Annual Rate and shows the cost of borrowing on an
annual basis, taking into account the effect of compounding.
