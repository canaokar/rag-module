# Customer Data Privacy Notice

## 1. Scope and Legal Basis

This privacy notice explains how the Bank collects, uses, shares, and
protects the personal data of its customers. It is provided in accordance
with the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

The Bank acts as the data controller for customer personal data. The Data
Protection Officer may be contacted at dpo@bank.example.com.

## 2. Categories of Personal Data

2.1 The Bank collects and processes the following categories of customer
personal data:

- Identity data: full name, date of birth, nationality, national insurance
  number, passport or identity document details
- Contact data: home address, telephone numbers, email addresses
- Financial data: income, employment details, credit history, account
  balances, transaction history
- Product data: details of products and services held, application history,
  correspondence
- Technical data: IP address, browser type, device information (when using
  digital channels)
- Marketing data: communication preferences, marketing consent records

## 3. Purposes and Lawful Bases

3.1 The Bank processes customer data for the following purposes:

- Contract performance: account management, payment processing, lending
  decisions, product servicing
- Legal obligation: AML and KYC checks, sanctions screening, tax reporting
  (CRS, FATCA), regulatory reporting
- Legitimate interests: fraud prevention, credit risk assessment, service
  improvements, analytics, debt recovery
- Consent: direct marketing via email and SMS, sharing data with selected
  partners

## 4. Automated Decision-Making

4.1 The Bank uses automated decision-making, including profiling, for the
following purposes: credit scoring, fraud detection, and AML transaction
monitoring.

4.2 Customers have the right to request human intervention in automated
decisions, to express their point of view, and to contest the decision.

4.3 Credit decisions are based on information provided by the customer,
data held by credit reference agencies, and the Bank's internal scoring
models.

## 5. Data Sharing

5.1 Customer data may be shared with the following categories of recipients:

- Credit reference agencies (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion)
- Fraud prevention agencies (CIFAS, National Hunter)
- Payment network operators (Visa, Mastercard, SWIFT)
- Regulatory authorities (FCA, PRA, HMRC, NCA)
- Debt collection agencies (where accounts are in arrears)
- Group companies (for intra-group service provision)

5.2 The Bank will not sell customer personal data to third parties.

## 6. International Transfers

6.1 Customer data may be transferred outside the UK where necessary for
payment processing or where group companies are located in other
jurisdictions.

6.2 All international transfers are subject to the Bank's Cross-Border Data
Transfer Standard and appropriate safeguards.

## 7. Retention

7.1 Customer personal data is retained for the duration of the customer
relationship plus 7 years following account closure, unless a longer
retention period is required by law.

7.2 Marketing consent records are retained for 2 years following the
withdrawal of consent.

## 8. Customer Rights

8.1 Customers have the following rights: right of access, right to
rectification, right to erasure, right to restrict processing, right to
data portability, right to object, and rights related to automated
decision-making.

8.2 Requests may be submitted via the Bank's website, in branch, or by
writing to the Data Protection Officer. The Bank will respond within 25
business days.

## 9. Cookies and Digital Tracking

9.1 The Bank's website and mobile applications use cookies and similar
technologies. Full details are provided in the Bank's Cookie Policy.

9.2 Essential cookies are used without consent. Analytics and marketing
cookies require the customer's consent via the cookie consent banner.

## 10. Governance and Oversight

The Data Protection Officer is responsible for monitoring compliance with
this notice and with the UK GDPR.

## 11. Review and Amendment

This notice must be reviewed at least annually or following a material change
in customer data processing practices.
