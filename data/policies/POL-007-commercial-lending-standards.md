# Commercial Lending Standards

## 1. Purpose

This policy governs the origination, underwriting, and management of lending
products across the Bank's commercial banking portfolio. It ensures that
credit risk is taken within approved risk appetite boundaries.

## 2. Credit Approval Authority

2.1 Individual commercial lending up to GBP 250,000 may be approved by
Relationship Managers with delegated authority.

2.2 Lending between GBP 250,001 and GBP 2,000,000 requires approval from the
Regional Credit Committee.

2.3 Exposures exceeding GBP 2,000,000 must be referred to the Group Credit
Committee with a full credit memorandum and independent risk opinion.

## 3. Underwriting Standards

3.1 Debt service coverage ratio must be at least 1.25x for term loans and
1.15x for revolving credit facilities.

3.2 Maximum leverage ratio (total debt to EBITDA) must not exceed 4.0x for
investment-grade borrowers and 3.0x for sub-investment-grade borrowers.

3.3 Commercial lending must be supported by cashflow projections covering the
full term of the facility plus a 15.0% stress buffer.

3.4 Financial covenants must be included in all facilities exceeding
GBP 500,000, including minimum interest coverage and maximum leverage ratios.

## 4. Collateral and Security

4.1 Acceptable collateral includes: commercial real property, plant and
equipment, receivables, inventory (subject to appropriate haircuts), listed
securities (subject to a haircut of 20.0%), and government bonds.

4.2 Independent valuations are required for all real property collateral
exceeding GBP 750,000.

4.3 Personal guarantees are required from directors holding 25% or more of
the borrowing entity where the entity has been trading for less than 3 years.

## 5. Portfolio Monitoring

5.1 The commercial credit portfolio must be reviewed monthly, with concentration
limits monitored against the approved Risk Appetite Statement.

5.2 Sector concentration limits are set at 15% of the total commercial lending
book for any single industry sector.

5.3 Early warning indicators must be reported to the Chief Risk Officer within
4 business days of detection.

## 6. Governance and Oversight

The Board of Directors retains ultimate responsibility for the matters covered
by this document. Day-to-day oversight is delegated to the relevant Executive
Committee member as specified in the Bank's Governance Map.

The policy owner must present a summary of compliance status, key metrics, and
emerging risks to the relevant Board sub-committee at least semi-annually.

## 7. Review and Amendment

This document must be reviewed at least annually or following a material change
in regulation, business strategy, or risk profile. The review must be documented
and signed off by the policy owner.

Amendments must follow the Bank's Document Change Control Procedure. All staff
affected by changes must be notified within 14 business days and must acknowledge
receipt.

## 8. Related Documents

This document should be read in conjunction with the Bank's Risk Appetite
Statement, the Credit Risk Policy, the Secured Lending Collateral Policy, and
the relevant PRA supervisory statements.
