# Data Retention and Disposal Policy

## 1. Scope

This policy governs the creation, storage, retention, and disposal of all
Bank records, whether physical or electronic. It ensures compliance with
legal, regulatory, and business requirements.

## 2. Retention Periods

2.1 Customer account records must be retained for a minimum of 7 years from
account closure.

2.2 AML and KYC records must be retained for at least 5 years from the end
of the business relationship.

2.3 Board and committee minutes must be retained permanently.

2.4 Tax records and returns must be retained for a minimum of 6 years from
the end of the relevant tax year.

2.5 Employment records must be retained for a minimum of 6 years from the
date of termination.

2.6 Contracts and legal agreements must be retained for a minimum of 6 years
from the expiry or termination of the agreement, or 12 years where the
contract is executed as a deed.

## 3. Legal Holds

3.1 Where litigation, regulatory investigation, or audit proceedings are
anticipated or in progress, a legal hold must be placed on all relevant
records.

3.2 Legal holds suspend the normal retention and disposal schedule until the
hold is released by the Legal function.

3.3 The Legal function must notify the Records Management team of all legal
holds within 2 business days.

## 4. Disposal

4.1 Records that have exceeded their retention period must be disposed of
securely within 45 business days unless subject to a legal hold.

4.2 Electronic records must be permanently deleted using approved data
destruction methods that comply with NCSC guidelines.

4.3 Physical records must be cross-cut shredded to DIN 66399 Level P-4 or
higher.

4.4 Certificates of destruction must be obtained and retained for all
disposals of Confidential or Restricted records.

## 5. Classification and Storage

5.1 All records must be classified according to the Bank's Data Classification
Standard at the point of creation.

5.2 Physical records classified as Confidential or Restricted must be stored
in secure, access-controlled locations.

5.3 Electronic records must be stored in approved systems with appropriate
access controls, backup, and disaster recovery.

## 6. Third-Party Records

6.1 Records held by third parties on behalf of the Bank are subject to the
same retention and disposal requirements.

6.2 Outsourcing contracts must include provisions for the return or secure
destruction of records upon contract termination.

## 7. Governance and Oversight

The Records Management function is responsible for maintaining the Retention
Schedule and monitoring compliance. The DPO provides oversight on data
protection aspects.

## 8. Review and Amendment

This document must be reviewed at least annually or following a material
change in legal or regulatory requirements. The review must be documented
and signed off by the policy owner.

## 9. Related Documents

This document should be read in conjunction with the Data Protection Policy,
the Information Security Policy, and the Legal Hold Procedure.
